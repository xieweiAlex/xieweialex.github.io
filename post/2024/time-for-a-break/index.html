In the Backdrop

So I’ve been in the workforce for 10+ years, since I graduated from college. Well, I did take a little break for around 6 months to prepare for the GRE exam and come to the US. But they are not break, let alone vacation or reset. The preparation and Master program were intense, and I didn’t have time nor energy to step back and analyze my situations.

And that’s when I realized how much I love Linux. Doing all that research and learning that I yearned for years was a great experience. I miss it.

It’s been 8+ years since I first started my journey in the US. While I’m proud of how far I’ve come in terms of my English, financial stability, and overall professional skills. I have to admit, I’m not completely satisfied with where I am.

Career Path

I’ve loved every role, but the last couple of years, I’ve been itching to dive into the world of Bitcoin, crypto, Finance/DeFi and cyber security. Time for a new adventure! Actually there have been a couple of opportunities, but I don’t have the needed skills, which it’s a shame. ü§∑

I often kick myself for not making important decisions sooner. Take my recent switching to Backend job position, for example. I had the feeling I would love it, even though it’s quite challenging. And guess what? I was right. I enjoy it so much that I have never thought about going back, but it took me a solid 5 years of self-doubting, yearning and hesitating before I took the plunge.

I have always been a wimp. I can admit it.

I’ve never been a go-getter, instead I kept wondering when the best opportunity, but during all that time, I wasn’t even preparing for it. Duh!! üò¶

So, why didn’t I just brush up on my Backend skills in my free time? Well, to be honest, I have quite some other interests that keep me pretty occupied.

And also, I’m not the type of person who can lock him/her self in a room for hours and forget about the world, I’m quite modest by nature, maybe that’s just an excuse.

Making a big career change requires a lot of hard work and dedication, and I don’t think I could handle that while juggling a full-time job. I could keep wondering “what if” for years and end up regretting it.

Life & Relationship

As I’m getting older, I started yearning for a settled life with a stable relationship. However, I don’t see it happening in the US for me anytime soon. It’s no secret that Asian men, especially immigrants, face challenges in the dating scene here. But that’s a whole other topic.

Being single definitely has its perks. I’ve had plenty time to entertain myself, and I have learned a lot about who I am and what I look for. I’ve found peace and contentment in my own company, which I believe is crucial. But I’m ready for a change.

I’m open to exploring new places where I might have better luck. I’ve already embraced the nomadic lifestyle after all.

New Opportunities

I aspire to become a global citizen, appreciating both American and Asian cultures. It is only natural for me to desire to live in various Asian countries (besides China) before settling down in the US.

Southeast Asia is a vibrant region with a large population, yet it is not as developed as other parts of the world. This presents a plethora of opportunities, in my opinion. With China’s remarkable economic growth over the past 30 years, and its recent slowdown, it is likely that many opportunities will arise in the southern region.

At the very least, I could establish a software development team there to take advantage of the lower labor costs.

Also I’m very interested in algorithmic trading, especially for crypto, I have been trading/investing in stock market for years, I really enjoy it, there are so much technical analysis skills that I need to learn and holding me back right now, I can see myself being a quant one day and proud of it.

However, I have to admit there are so much for me to learn in terms of technical analysis skills, which is holding me back. Nevertheless, I can see my passion towards it, and the huge opportunity, I can become a successful quant in future and that would be so much fun.

In the end, tie those loose ends

Aside from all of those serious matters, I have a long list of books that I would like to finish reading, as well as numerous tools that I would like to focus on more, such as Beancount, Vim (Nvim), Anki, and Sparrow wallet.

I am also tired of being a consumer. I yearn to actively participate in projects and make a meaningful contribution. The concept of building in public is intriguing and I am eager to embark on this journey. I’m ready for it! ‚úä
