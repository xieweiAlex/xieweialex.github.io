Active vs passive learning

I have been reading the book “Poor Charlie’s Almanac” this month, thanks MoneyXYZ online book club, otherwise I would not have the motivation to read so dilligently.

I realized difference between “Active and Passive learning”, when we listen to podcast or watch Youtube video, it’s relaxing and we just follow the lead of the thinking journey.

But it’s not very efficient, as it’s a one-size-fits-all approach. We all learn very differently for various reasons, some concepts might be easy for some people but hard to others, and vice versa. It’s very important to take a pause and take notes in learning, and most importantly, learning requires getting back and regurgitate the content(at least for most people). You can argue most of media content we consume on-demand, we can pause and rewind, but to be honest, I don’t think most people do it. Comparing with reading, we do it all the time.

I’ve been using Readwise for quite a while, I find the spaced repetition method working for me amazingly well. I knew the concept of “Active vs Passive Learning” very early on, but I didn’t think deep about it. Thanks MoneyXYZ online reading club let me realize it.

Delight

Reader

I’ve heard a lot of raves about Reader, since I’m already a Readwise paid user, I decided to give it a try. It’s such a good product, well designed, short-cut oriented, multi-functional, I love it so much!

More importantly, I salvaged myself from the endless email newsletter, I love those email letters, but the issue is I can’t finish reading them, I had been agonized by the endless email list and most of time just blindly mark them as “Read” to keep my sanity. This is a life saver, I’m so glad I found it.

LazyVim

I have been using Vim for almost a decade( Vim and Neovim), as a self-professed heavy Vim user, I actually don’t see much difference from them. But moving to Lazyvim is big deal, even though everything is still vim based, but it’s highly pre-configured, it’s like a betrayal to the community spirit. But I have to face it, I don’t have time to configure everything myself, the last decade I didn’t finish it, as I get busier and busier, I need to preserve my time.
