Looking for a long-term partner

Alex Xie

December 29, 2022

  • relationship
  • Romance

How it started

I’ve been alone for a while, and I’m getting old(30+), I started feeling the urge to have a “partner in crime”, AKA, feeling lonely. So I asked around, and people have always asked back “what kind of partner are you looking for? " I hate not being able to articulate what’s in my mind, so I figured I could write it down, since there is nothing secretive about it.

Basically I’m looking for a girl who is just like me in a lot of ways, ideally of course, having similar values and lifestyles, I believe that’s what makes a relationship successful.

Disclaimer: I’m aware in real life lots of rules / preferences would be overshadowed when there is chemistry happening, but we can still take it as a grain of salt.

Values I look for

Positive vibes

I’m an optimistic person by nature, and I value it, I worked hard on it. I live by hope, as it opposes to fear or worry. I strive to be better day by day, and I’m pretty happy to see what I’ve gone through over the years.

It’s hard for me to work/live too closely with negative or pessimistic people, Even though I know their probably have valid points, but if there are no actionable items or alternative, some whinings could only be useful in certain limit.

Receptive / Open-minded

I’m from China, and as much as I love American/Western culture, I can’t deny that deep down I still hold a lot of value from Asian roots. I think ideally she is an Asian descendant or at least into Asian culture. (ps. I need to write another an article about what I see the difference between Asian culture vs American culture.)

I’m quite open-minded, and I like to learn new things(tools/tips/theories), and anything related to productivity and self-improvement. Not necessarily my partner needs to onboard with whatever I pursue, but it would be great if she has similar mindset, we can learn and grow together.

For example, in the year 2022, I’ve embarked on quite some explorations. I see myself would stick with them for quite some time.
Fermentation (Sauerkraut, Kombucha, kimchi, yogurt, etc), MBTI (personality test), and stoicism (ancient Greek philosophy), just to name a few. I’ve probably invested 100+ hours in each of them, and I can’t stop myself from talking about them.

I like to apply to what I’ve learned to daily life, it truly make me happy. But those practice often conflicts with traditions, I don’t always understand why people stick with traditions when they know it’s wrong or there is a better way. Though I know changing is hard.

For example, I had been eating white rice my whole life, and I’ve let it out of my diet because I’d like to cut off carbs.(besides eating out with others). I don’t eat breakfast (I do 16:8 intermittent fasting, breakfast is not the most important meal of the day), just to new a few.

Supportive / Empathetic / Feminine

Even though I’m not a Feeler person, (I ascribe this to my relative rough upbringing), but I always appreciate people being supportive/considerate/empathetic, she doesn’t need to be very smart, ambitious or even strong, I’d like to take the more traditional masculine part of responsibility.

I’m not a macho person in any sense, on the contrary, I see myself as a mild feminist.

Lifestyles I look for

Intuition vs Sensing

This is MBTI’s parlance, I’m a “Ni” (Introverted Intuitive) person, I’m drawn into abstract things, as opposed to tangible items.

For example, my typical table topics are more often related to economy, business, habit(personal growth), psychology, productivity tools etc. And I often found myself shrugging off at those material, sensual things or experiences- things like fine dining, clothing, partying, even excessive cleaning.

Bringing this up not to put it as a requirement but to seek understanding, I know lots of people are not like me, and I surely understand. I hope my partner understands my interest as well.

Emotion Stability

I’m quite emotionally stable, and I value it. I see so many people who are not, and they’re not even working on it, nor see it as a problem, that’s a big no-no to me.

I believe in Stoicism(an ancient Greek philosophy), as Nasim Taleb put it perfectly, Stoicism is about “the domestication of emotions, not their elimination.”

Maintaining a consistent light mood is essential for me, I don’t expect everyone, myself included, could achieve that easily, we could work on that at least.

Money

I live a quite frugal life comparing with most people around me, I often joke about that my spending ability is like a new grad. I think it’s the right thing to do, I have low desire for material stuff. I always try to safe money, as long as it doesn’t hurt my health and take too much time, I see it as a virtue.

So I probably won’t be happy with people who enjoy extravagant lifestyle. I don’t mean we can’t indulge ourselves with some worldly pressure some times, but just do it consciously and less frequently.

I’m a big believer in investing, and currently I’m mostly invested in risky assets(stocks and crypto), and I would gradually lean to safer assets like Real Estate and bonds. I hope my partner would not be a risk-averse person, because I enjoy take calculated risks.

Active(Physically and mentally)

I like all kinds of sports and physical exercises, I work hard to maintain a fit body. I hope my partner would enjoy physical activity to some extend, and we can do some group exercises like hiking, jogging, swimming, or kayaking etc in spare time.

I like to learn new things(theories, tools, ideas or even recipes for food), I’d love to share those things to my partner.

At the end

I’m not a saint, I’m naturally attracted to good-looking girls like any other dude. But I always know those traits would only get us started, and personality, value and lifestyles alignment would keep us going for a long term.

There are could be lost of things I miss from my logical mind, I would be very grateful if you let me know about them.
