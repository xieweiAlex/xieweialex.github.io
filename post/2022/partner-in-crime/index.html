Looking for a long-term partner

Alex Xie

December 29, 2022

  • relationship
  • looking

How it started

As I’ve grown older (30+), I’ve found myself spending more and more time alone. While I value my independence, I can’t help but feel a longing for a partner in crime to share my life with. The feeling of loneliness has started to creep in, and I’m ready to find someone to join me on this journey.

So I asked around in my friend circle, I often get asked the question, “what kind of partners are you searching for?” It frustrates me when I can’t articulate my thoughts. So I figured I could write them down, since there is nothing secretive about it.

Basically I’m looking for a girl who shares some similarities with me, particularly in terms of values and lifestyle, I believe that’s what makes a relationship successful.

Values I look for

Positive Vibes

I am naturally an optimistic person and I value this trait. I have worked hard to cultivate it.

I find it challenging to work or live with negative or pessimistic people. While I understand that they may have valid concerns, constant complaining without offering solutions or taking action can be draining. I do my fair share of venting, but I am actively trying to improve and be more positive.

Receptive / Open-minded / learn and change

I came from a country on the other side of the Pacific Ocean. While I have a deep appreciation for American and Western culture, I cannot deny that my Asian heritage holds a special place in my heart. Ideally, I would love to find someone who shares a similar background or is at least interested in Asian culture.

PS. I would like to write an article discussing the differences between Asian culture and American culture.

I have a curious and open-minded nature, always eager to expand my knowledge and skills through learning new tools, tips, and theories. I am particularly interested in topics related to productivity and self-improvement, constantly seeking ways to enhance my personal growth. It is not a requirement for my partner to support my pursuits, but it would be beautiful if we share a similar mindset and can support each other’s growth and learning.

In the year 2022, I have embarked on a journey of exploration. I have delved into various interests, which I plan to continue for the foreseeable future. These include fermentation, such as making sauerkraut, kombucha, kimchi, and yogurt etc. As well as studying MBTI and Carl Jung’s personality theories, and delving into the ancient Greek philosophy of Stoicism. I have dedicated over 100 hours to each of these pursuits, and I find myself constantly eager to discuss them with others.

I find joy in applying what I’ve learned to my daily life. However, I often encounter conflicts with traditional practices. I struggle to understand why people continue to adhere to traditions when they know they are wrong or there is a better alternative. I understand that change can be difficult though.

For instance, I have always eaten white rice, but I have recently cut it out of my diet in an effort to reduce carbs intake (except when eating out with others). I also do not eat breakfast, as I follow a 16:8 intermittent fasting schedule (contrary to the belief that breakfast is the most important meal of the day). These are just a few examples of how I incorporate my learning into my daily routine.

Supportive / Empathetic / Feminine

Despite not being a Feeler(In MBTI’s parlance), I believe my upbringing has made me less attuned to emotions. However, I highly value people who are supportive, considerate, and empathetic. Intelligence, ambition, and even strength are not necessary for me, as I am happy to take on the traditional masculine role of responsibility.

I do not consider myself a macho person though, I see myself as a mild feminist.

Lifestyles I look for

Intuition

In MBTI’s parlance again: I am an “Ni” (Introverted Intuitive) person, meaning I am naturally drawn to abstract concepts rather than tangible objects.

For example, my typical table topics often center around topics like macro/micro economy, technology, business, personal growth, and psychology. I tend to overlook discussions about material possessions/obsession or sensory experiences, such as fine dining, sightseeing, beauty, partying etc.

Bringing this up not to put it as a requirement but to seek understanding, I know lots of people are not like me, and I understand. I hope my partner understands my interest as well.

Emotional Stability

I am happy to say that I am emotionally stable and I place a high value on it. It is disheartening to see people who struggle with their emotions.

I find solace in learning Stoicism, an ancient Greek philosophy. As Nasim Taleb eloquently stated, Stoicism is “the domestication of emotions, not their elimination.”

Maintaining a consistent light mood critically important for me. I understand that it may not be easy for everyone, myself included, to achieve this consistently. Let’s work together to improve on this aspect.

Money

I have always been known for my frugal lifestyle, often joking that my spending habits are more in line with a recent college graduate. But for me, it’s a conscious and responsible choice. I have little interest in material possessions and instead prioritize saving money. As long as it doesn’t affect my health or take up too much of my time, I see it as a virtue.

I may not be fond of individuals who constantly seek out a lavish lifestyle. While I understand the occasional desire to indulge in worldly pleasures, I believe it should be done consciously and in moderation.

I strongly believe in the power of investing and currently have a significant portion of my portfolio allocated to high-risk assets such as stocks and cryptos(Bitcoin). However, as I continue to grow and diversify my investments, I plan to gradually shift towards more stable assets like Real Estate and bonds. It’s important to me that my partner shares a similar mindset and is not too much afraid to take calculated risks.

Active (Physically and mentally)

I am passionate about staying active and maintaining a healthy lifestyle through various sports and physical exercises. I am looking for a partner who also values physical activity and would be open to joining me in activities such as hiking, jogging, swimming, kayaking etc, and more during our free time.

I like to learn new things(theories, tools, ideas or even recipes for food), I’d love to share those things to my partner.

I’m also passionate about learning and expanding my knowledge, whether it be through exploring new theories, tools, or new recipes. I find great joy in sharing my discoveries with my partner and am always excited to see their reaction.

Physical appearance

I’m not a saint, and just like any other guy, I’m attracted to good-looking girls. However, I understand that those superficial traits don’t matter much in a relationship. What truly matters is compatibility in terms of personality, values, and lifestyle, which are the foundations for a lasting connection.

In the end

There you have it! These are the things I can think of for a successful relationship for me. Of course, the list is not exhaustive, but we can start from here.
