My life principles after 20s (version 0.1 in Mar 31 2020)

As I’m a lifetime learner turned 30 years old, I’ve learned a lot. I’ve found some life principles work for me very well and I’d like to write them down to inspect and review them regularly.

Embrace English

I can’t emphasis the importance of English since I’m living a country English is the official language, English ability would still one of my bottleneck for in few years. It takes time, I can’t do it overnight or over a year.

I’ve always been fascinate by the information abundance in English, besides that, all the tools and programming languages are in English, most of all, since I write everything in Vim and English in Vim is the best I can get for now. As I’m looking to fit in or understand more about the Western culture, language plays a big part in culture (so does food, but I don’t see myself would into it, I’m so proud the tasty, delicacy and abundance of Asian food ðŸ™‚)

I’ve had a hard time learning English during my youth and adult life, I’ve spent tons of hours learning it on purposely in my adult time which I think it would be more fun and higher ROI in other things in a short term. I’d like to have my kid growing up in a decent English speaking environment and it would be one of the biggest gift I leave for him/her.

Embrace digitalization

Everything could be digitalized will be digitalized, and we’re just getting started. Since I’m working on the edge of the revolution, I’m so excited every time it comes to my mind, I’m very grateful I was accidentally get into the battlefield, and I’ve been benefiting a lot, and I will try to get more. ðŸ˜ƒ

sunlight is the best disinfectant

I heavily believe transparency is the solution for lots of things I don’t understand, as long as they’re transparent, we can hold them accountable, I’m more open to it.

Anything worth doing worthy doing publicly.

I’m not into bombshells. Like burying yourself into a big, grandiose goal and hope you can make it someday and stun the world, that’s just not my style. Just like agile philosophy in software programming, I’d like to gather feedback as soon as possible, and do retrospective regularly, fast iteration and loop.

Use tools to make our life better

Lots of things are just tools make us better, either physically or digitally.

Tools change my mind how things can be done and should be done, it’s infra.

future oriented

bottom of my heart, I don’t have much sense of security, I got the comfortness and assurance by awaring it gonna work in a long term.

I don’t like sacrifice.

delay gratification

sustainable for a long term

Be happy and be nice

One of big part in western culture I like is it emphasis indivisiual personal happiness and achievement, I’d like to always care for myself, ask myself what do I really want, what makes me happy and do things I think it’s right, then I will be happy living consciously.

I’m a happy person I think most people around me can feel, I can be happy easily for little small things in life, that’s just my nature. But as big things, in a big picture, how do I make myself happy?

Well, there are few things make me happy in general, political correct(I’m a typical leftish), I feel cool about what I do, I feel myself strong both in mentally and physically, I earn more than I expend, I know what I’m doing and it’s sustainable, etc.

Current struggle is the planning missing days/years ago, prevent it from happening again.

Life is imperfect, deal with it, some regret make your life more memorable(regardless the result might change, they might not true).
